How-to Guides

How to Propagate a Pothos Plant

Follow these simple steps to propagate a Pothos plant:

  1. Choose a healthy vine with several leaves.
  2. Identify a node (the small bump where roots grow).
  3. Use clean scissors to cut the vine just below the node.
  4. Remove the bottom leaves near the cutting.
  5. Place the cutting in a glass of water, ensuring the node is submerged.
  6. Position the glass in bright, indirect light.
  7. Change the water every 5-7 days to prevent stagnation.
  8. After 2-4 weeks, roots should appear.
  9. Once the roots are a few inches long, transfer the cutting into soil.
  10. Water the new plant and watch it grow.

Indoor Plant Facts

Peace Lilies are known for filtering common toxins like benzene and formaldehyde from the air, improving indoor air quality.

Indoor plants can help reduce stress and create a calming atmosphere, making them great for workspaces and homes.

Pothos can thrive in water alone. Simply place cuttings in a jar of water and they'll continue to grow with regular water changes.

Monstera deliciosa has natural holes in its leaves, called fenestrations, which help light pass through and improve air circulation in rainforests.

Snake Plants are unique as they release oxygen during the night, making them ideal for bedrooms and promoting better sleep quality.
